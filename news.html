Soweto Home Based Care feeding scheme relaunch's in Jabavu

Soweto HBC has been doing a remarkable job in saving needy citizens.Amongest other things, the center has been feeding kids from disadvantaged families and also went beyond that to have after-school programme where the caregivers would assist the kids with their school work. With unfortunate financial constraits, the center had to be shut down which left the beneficiaries stranded. With a biblical saying- stating that God can never abandoned his kids,Evolve Executive Search firm and Diakonia AIDS Ministry came to rescue the center. On the two parties' agreement, the center has now adopted by Diakonia Ministry and all services are back to normal.Evolve's Corporal Social Investment Manager Tshifhiwa Mpho Mughivi says this was the right thing to do. "Every child has a right to be loved, and love is only found at home as such these kids must have a place called home, and this is their home.As part of Kotula, a community uplifting program from Evolve, this was necessary."Lets show these kids some love,let them feel at home again.We don't have much,but with extra hands from local businesses and the community at large we can make a difference, explains Mughivi."I would like to encourage everybody to learn to live with one another,support each other and show one another love.Lets show these kids some love because it is what they learn from early ages that will reflect when they become adults.If we can show them love now, they will be able to do the same to others when they are adults," indicates Lindiwe Mqedlana from Botshelo Arthritis. "It feels good knowing that there will be no kids going to bed with empty stomach.Foremost, this is not about food but also education.Our legend Madiba stated that education is a key to open all doors and a great weapon to face life's challenges," says Maribe Mamabolo.
